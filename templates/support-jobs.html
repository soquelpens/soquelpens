Every family enrolled at Soquel PENS takes on one support job for the school year. Jobs are assigned based on your preferences — fill out the Support Jobs Form during registration and we'll do our best to match you with a role you'll love.
Leadership Roles

Board of Directors

Our Board of Directors is made up entirely of parent volunteers. Board members attend monthly meetings, make decisions about school operations, and are essential to keeping our non-profit running. Each Board role counts as your support job for the year.

Oversees all school operations and Board meetings. Serves as liaison between families, teachers, and WASCAE. Signs contracts and represents the school in official matters. Supports the President and steps in when needed. Often manages member communications, class meetings, and assists with administrative tasks throughout the year. Records and distributes minutes from all Board and class meetings. Maintains official school records and ensures meeting notices go out on time. Manages all school finances including tuition collection, bill payment, and financial reporting to the Board and WASCAE. Works with families on payment questions. First point of contact for prospective families. Manages the enrollment waitlist, offers spots, and guides new families through the registration process. Attends monthly WASCAE board meetings representing Soquel PENS. Communicates relevant information back to our school community.
Classroom Support

Classroom & daily operations

These jobs support what happens inside our classroom every day — from keeping supplies stocked to capturing memories of our children at play.

Takes candid photos of children during class days and special events. Organizes and shares photos with families. Coordinates the end-of-year photo book or slideshow. Creates and distributes the monthly snack schedule for families. Sends reminders and ensures all families understand the nutritious snack guidelines. Monitors classroom art supplies and coordinates purchasing replacements with the Treasurer. Keeps the art area organized and well-stocked throughout the year. Makes fresh homemade playdough weekly and delivers it to school. A beloved job — children always notice when a new batch arrives! Recipe provided. Manages the classroom lending library. Tracks which books have been checked out, sends gentle reminders for returns, and keeps the library organized and inviting. Monitors and restocks cleaning and household supplies used at school — paper towels, soap, cleaning sprays, and similar items. Coordinates purchases with the Treasurer.
Grounds & Maintenance

Our outdoor environment

Maintaining our beautiful outdoor play area and school building is a shared responsibility. These roles keep our campus safe, clean, and welcoming for all our children and families.

Tends to the school garden, coordinates planting with the teacher, organizes families for garden work parties, and ensures the outdoor space is safe and enriching for children. Assists with school repairs, painting, building projects, and general upkeep. Works alongside other families during scheduled work parties each semester. Organizes and leads the school's semester clean-up days. Delegates tasks to parent volunteers, ensures deep cleaning happens twice a year, and coordinates with the teacher. Conducts monthly safety walkthroughs of the outdoor play area and school building. Identifies and reports hazards, checks playground equipment, and ensures a safe environment.
Fundraising & Community

Building community together

Our annual events bring families together and raise the funds that keep tuition affordable. Every enrolled family contributes 4 hours of fundraising time and a minimum of $100 raised per year.

Plans and executes our beloved annual Fall Carnival — our biggest community fundraiser. Coordinates volunteers, vendors, games, and activities for the whole family. Organizes our cozy annual Soup & Story Night fundraiser dinner. Coordinates donations, prepares the space, and creates a warm community gathering for families. Organizes our annual Read-A-Thon fundraiser. Sends materials to families, tracks pledges, follows up with donors, and celebrates our readers at the finish. Manages our school's RaiseRight (formerly eScrip) gift card program. Onboards new families, tracks earnings, and communicates regular updates about participating retailers. Manages the BOGO Jamba Juice card program. Purchases cards, distributes to families and the community, and tracks proceeds from each card sold on behalf of the school. Plans our spring community celebration. Coordinates with families, secures a venue or organizes the school space, and ensures a memorable end-of-year event.
Communications

Keeping our community connected

These roles help us communicate with current families, prospective parents, and the broader Santa Cruz community.

Maintains the school calendar and ensures all events, work parties, and important dates are communicated to families well in advance. Sends reminders as needed. Collects content from teachers, parents, and board members and compiles the monthly school newsletter. Distributes to all families and keeps archives organized. Manages the school's Instagram and Facebook presence. Shares classroom moments, event photos, and enrollment information while respecting family privacy guidelines. Keeps the school website current — updating enrollment information, events, forms, and resources. Basic computer skills required; training and support provided.
